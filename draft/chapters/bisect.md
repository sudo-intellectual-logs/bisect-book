Bisect
Zachary Abrahamson

The civilization they live in has almost all of it's core social infrastructure as an integrated enterprise system which runs smoothly and has a high 'social uptime' The protagonist is one of the evaluators for the system's functioning. Essentially, a general purpose human in the loop that mostly just hangs out because there isn't much to do. Every nation (repo) has an evaluator like this, but the protagonist is at hq so he sees the full system. 

But there start to be unexpected bugs they need to hunt down and 'fix' but those bugs start to cause other bugs (ie social unrest, poverty, sickness, etc.. all kinds of ills) they then realize that this is a problem, send someone to the field on a (code) inspection tour to do root cause analysis but then there's an inflection point and there are too many bugs to count so they have to triage, they initiate bisect protocol and send an evaluator to each step to figure out what's going on in repos where things are still great and where things are really bad. 

The story is told through the evaluator left behind to be the watch officer who sees through their eyes telling their story in bits and pieces from what he sees while managing the day to day at the office as it's a skeleton crew and everyone is gone besides him, the crusty old sysadmin, the janitor, and the guard etc...

After the search, Half the field evaluators are able to fix their repo but the good repos flip bad so they're in the same problem. The sysadmin wants to checkout from the last safe backup repo, but it would imply wiping out the development of the civilizational repos although the people will presumably(?) be fine.

From seeing the bits and pieces of everyone's story, V.M, the watch evaluator, realizes but can't explain that everything would be great if you just merged the aspects from the repos together, and recommends a commit to merge everything. The sysadmin hates, says it'll be a mess and create chaos. And reprimands him only for Logan to try to it anyway. Of course, it requires admin privileges and sysadmin is notified, is furious, and takes away his credential/token whatever.

As he explains, poorly, why merging would fix everything, the team is convinced that it's the right call and form a plan to steal the sysadmins credential which he carelessly leaves but his desk when he goes to the the bathroom. Since Logan is tied up, the office jokester decides to go, make the commit from the sysadmin station. Logan asks him to use his creds and gives him his passcode so no one else gets in trouble. 

The trickster gets logans creds, initiates the commit, grabs the sysadmin creds, needs the passcode, guesses the equivalent of 'password' a couple times before the loginattempts run out and eventual guesses it since it's all 6s or 7s or whatever and launches it before sneaking back. 

Once the sysadmin gets back and sees what happened to the repos that have merged he flips out, stops the process, and tries to revert the merge but it's too late, the merged 1/3 of repos are essentially chaos while the others still have problems. He turns to Logan (we'll actually put him in the office in 'prison') and demands the credential and password to initiate backup. Maybe there's something like the terminals are far enough away you need two people and Logan helped the trickster by inputting the code with his tongue or something a little weird like that?

Defeated and guilty about his plan not working, Logan complies with the the sysadmin, except he absentmindedly puts in his personal  credentials (for spending money, id etc...) versus his work credentials and it won't accept his pin which he inputs enough times to lock the system out.

The sysadmin gets even more furious, calling the act intentional treason and calling a summary disciplinary review to determine whether he can be sentenced to death by bissection (robot mediated forced organ donation). 

They set up a trial where, after a period of preparation where Logan wallows in everything and the team get more and more remorseful about what they've done, they stage what's effectively a show trial where all but one of the 12 person team of field evaluators provide contorted testimony about how Logan skirted policy,, broke the rules, and otherwise undermined the good functioning of the system.

There are all kinds of ethical, moral, and other arguments about whether what logan did was justified and, if not, is at least redeemable. The sysadmin uses the extensive logs and coerced testimony to get the required authorizations to execute a bissection. And sysadmin takes Logan to the managing directors office to get The dusty old credential for an execution (there hasn't been one on quite a while) they take Logan to the dissection chamber, he agonizes over how he's going to die. He's given a three drug cocktail to have him die before, but the sysadmin out of spite leaves out the fatal component so Logan will have to do not live.

The sysadmin goes to start the procedure, authorizes his, Logan's identity and then uses the execution cred (three factor). Normally it would be the doctor, but it's too late and they're gone. But, as luck will have it,  the execution creds are expired and need to be manually renewed when the office opens in the morning. 

This gives the team  13 hours, 5pm to 8am, of time to talk through what's going on, mix their witness account, pair it with what Logan says in the trial and figure out the mix of heuristics they can use to fix the system they get at using recursion and some a multi optimizing strategy that balances their relative strengths and weaknesses dynamically and find the worst repo to test it in. They get it figured out over an hour while Logan watches over video link (the sysadmin set it up so they'd have to watch what happens to a traitor) but they're all just talking with each other and don't seem to care.

But. Having used an hour to solve within themselves, the coopt the monitoring infrastructure to send a message to 12 repos where each field evaluator facilitates conveying the, battlefield improvised, strategy to each repo, and each repo to 12 repos, and 12 more etc.. such that after the 12 hours all of the 8 trillion bugs in all the repos have been patched, at least for now. 

Then, after returning with a fresh execution credential, the sysadmin as about to execute the procedure for real but gets a call from the managing director saying the access has been revoked because everything is better now, so he can't kill him. But he doesn't tell that to Logan. He offers him a deal, he can live but has to spend his life in a terrible penal repo where he lives in and maintains the archival legacy system as forced labor for the rest of his life or he can choose dissection himself and provide the third authorization. 

Logan considers it, works it through, feeling abandoned and neglected and depressed and everything considers and makes up his mind to die. He does the first auth, sysadmin 2, but before Logan can do the last auth his girlfriend, the doctor arrives for work, and not bearing to have her watch. Renegs and takes the deal.

Sysadmin explains what happened in the least charitable way possible, and allows a last goodbye. She, overcome by emotion can only get out that she's pregnant, a virtual impossibility since everyone at system hq has a vasectomy and their tubes tied. Logan is overcome with joy and despair, knowing he will never see his child, but the sysadmin separates them as the girlfriend hands an envelope to Logan as he leaves.

Logan is now rushed to the transit station, packed on a train, given transfer orders. And sent to repo 0 where the conditions described are full of drudgery, menial, duplicative, and onerous tasks. Logan reads reflects on the report he wrote that said system functioning optimally, feature not a bug. Recommend merge time stamped before the whole ordeal. 

He Reflects on it, gives a sigh of detached acceptance and takes the report over to the recycle bin in the train car where he sees,  to his surprise, that there is a stack of similar, but not identical reports. Stunned, he's interrupted by the conductor who checks his orders and says. Ah, just another day in paradise isn't it. To which Logan doesn't know to respond. 

He sits sees his bedraggled reflection in the window, sees his eyes, bloodshot, the new wrinkles, and squints, he can just make out the giant eyes of the directorate headquarters. He always hated those. He felt like he was being watched. He then remembers the envelope, opens it and sees an ultrasound and labelled 10001: Eve and as he flips it to the pack and sees the label: ‘ls -e’ with a heart drawn around it. He looks back at his reflection, catches a glimpse of the horizon, and smiles.
