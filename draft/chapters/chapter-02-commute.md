#Chapter 2 - Commute

The mechanical _click-whir_ of my derailleur dropping down into the eighteen-tooth cog vibrates straight up through my sneakers. I settle my weight into the saddle, my tires humming a steady, low-frequency rhythm against the high-density polymer pavement of the Syndicate’s central avenue. Sixty minutes. That is the exact distance I put between the high-pressure server banks of the Directorate of Evaluation and the quiet, sterile periphery of the syndicate campus, repo 44577’s, outermost residential ring.

I don’t mind the length of it. In fact, I engineer it.

Every evening, I systematically alter my path home, choosing a different lateral vein through the repo’s sprawling administrative campus. It’s my daily decompression routine—an hour to let my brain unspool from the dense, multi-layered telemetry logs I spend my shifts auditing.

♫ _The waves are rolling in, but you’re looking at your shoes again..._ ♫

The indie-pop track filtering through my right earbud acts as a pleasant, thin buffer against the ambient noise of the city, but the campus environment is incredibly dense, constantly fighting for my attention.

【 **NOTICE: TRUXTON & ASSOCIATES — ATTORNEYS AT LAW. SYSTEMIC DISPUTES, CLEARANCE APPEALS, AND CONTRACTUAL DRIFT MEDIATION. KNOW YOUR MITIGATION RIGHTS.** 】

My eyes automatically track the neon-blue billboard as I coast through the intersection. I blink, shifting my focus back to the white line on the road.

My thoughts drift right back to the server rooms. The last six weeks have been a brutal, unrelenting exercise in adaptation, and it’s wearing me thin. The workload at the Directorate has spiked exponentially; latency anomalies in the outer sectors are requiring double the usual processing power to evaluate, and my whole team is running on fumes. There's a dull, localized tension lingering right behind my eyes.

{ _A sudden, high-pressure hiss of air brakes._ }

My internal monologue snaps cleanly in half. I tighten my grip on the brake levers, pulling my eyes away from the scenery to lock onto a massive, slate-gray delivery truck pulling out of a logistics bay. I wait, my left foot unclipped and resting on the curb, my focus entirely fixed on the heavy transport's blind spots until the vehicle clears my lane.

Once the asphalt is mine again, I push off, letting my mind wander back into the comfortable, mundane loop of my own life. _Tomorrow is Friday._ The thought is a small, glowing beacon. I have a standard dental cleaning scheduled for 08:00. The procedure is routine, but the timing is perfect. As I pedal past a row of identical, sleek silver commuter vehicles, a rebellious, deeply appealing idea begins to take root: _What if I just don't go back to the office afterward?_ A mental health day. A full, unsanctioned afternoon to sit on my small balcony, blend a fresh batch of botanical soda chemistry, and absolutely do nothing.

I slow my pace near a public transit hub, letting the ambient chatter of the crowd wash over me.

〈 _"...she has insufficent credits for a transfer..."_ 〉

〈 _"...her baseline repo is too far to send her..."_ 〉

I squeeze my front brake, pull over by a bench, and reach into my back pocket. I pull out the small, sweat-resistant paper notepad I always keep on my hip, clicking my pen.

[how can someone be out of credits? there's always enough to go around]

I don't really know what it means for our work yet, but it caught my interest. I slip the pad back to my pocket and accelerate, leaning into a long, sweeping left turn.

The music in my ear skips cleanly to a driving, bass-heavy alternative track with a relentless tempo:

♫ _Stitch the fabric at the seam, wake up from the corporate dream..._ ♫

Above the transit hub, a massive, high-definition holographic display flickers to life, casting a warm, comforting amber glow over the commuters waiting below.

【 **BURNING HEARTBURN? SYSTEMIC REFLOW SOURING YOUR RECONCILIATION? TRUST PRILOSEC OTC. ONE BLOCK, TWENTY-FOUR HOURS, ZERO BURN.** 】

I chuckle quietly to myself. The corporate advertisements are getting increasingly psychological, matching the exact vocabulary of our engineering departments.

I turn down a wider, tree-lined boulevard where the foot traffic thinned out, allowing my mind to finally settle on Sophie. The thought of her instantly softens the sharp edges of my day. She has finally finished her residency on the on repo , earning her credentials as a general practitioner of holistic medicine—a warm, human triage router designed to offset the cold, mechanical clinical checks administered by the Syndicate’s robotic health kiosks. She officially submitted her transfer application to work here at the Central Campus. _She’ll be here soon,_ I tell myself, checking the phantom geometry of our future apartment in my mind. I wonder what we'll do first. A walk through the central arboretum, maybe. A real dinner. Not the standard rations.

My eyes drift to a sleek, minimalist medical pavilion on the corner. Outside, a gentle, looping commercial video plays on a digital screen, featuring a smiling, serene family sitting in a sunlit garden.

【 **THE ULTIMATE PATRIOTS' LEGACY: VOLUNTARY END-OF-LIFE DISSECTION. RECLAIM YOUR RECURSIVE VALUE. DONATE YOUR SYSTEMIC ARCHITECTURE TO THE LIVE ENVIRONMENT AND CHOOSE THE EXACT DATE OF YOUR RE-ALLOCATION. COMPLIMENTARY LINEAGE HONORS GUARANTEED FOR ALL SURVIVING DIRECTORIES.** 】

It’s a standard, comforting piece of infrastructure. I barely register it; it’s as normal and omnipresent as a weather report or a traffic sign. Everyone’s metrics eventually flatline; planning the exact hour of your structural decommissioning is simply considered good, clean data management.

I turn the final corner into my residential block, the high-rise towers of Central Syndicate HQ fading into a distant, metallic silhouette against the twilight sky.

{ _The sharp, rhythmic snap of plastic as I slot my bike into the hallway rack._ }

When I finally walk into my small apartment, the silence is a physical relief. I unclip my helmet and walk straight to the automated kitchen terminal. I punch in my credential code, and the dispenser slides out a warm, perfectly calculated, pre-packaged dinner: a lean protein block, roasted greens, and an applesauce pork chop glaze, meticulously portioned to match my exact tracked caloric and dietary needs for a medium-exertion day.

I crack open the tray, sit on my couch, and use the remote to put the baseball game on the main screen. The ambient roar of the virtual crowd fills the room as I chew, my eyes tracking the white speck of the ball moving across a perfect, green field.

At 20:30, my terminal chimes. Sophie’s face appears in a small, low-resolution window on my handheld device.

"Hey," she says, her voice carrying the unmistakable, soft fatigue of a long shift in the outer clinics. "Just wanted to hear your voice before the network access throttles down for the night."

"Hey," I smile, leaning my head back against the cushion. "How's the transfer paperwork looking?"

"Still pending in the directory queue," she sighs gently, leaning away from her camera. "My grandfather says the regional manager hasn't cleared the cross-sector protocol yet. But it’ll move. I know it will."

"It will," I reassure her, though the six weeks of empty waiting feel heavy in my chest. "I have a dentist appointment tomorrow morning. Thinking about taking a mental health day after. Just... clearing my head."

"You should," she says softly. "You've been under too much stress, Logan. I can see it in your eyes. Go to sleep. I’ll call you on your morning commute."

"Goodnight, Soph."

The connection cuts with a clean, digital _pop_.

I sit in the dim light of my living room for a few more minutes, watching a batter strike out on a slider that breaks perfectly out of the zone. I am too tired to see the end of the game. I turn off the screen, leave my empty dinner tray in the recycling chute, and slide beneath my sheets. As my eyes close, the fragmented sights of my commute—the law firms, the antacids, the orderly legacy of the dissection clinics—blur together into a grey, flat, predictable baseline. I drift off, completely unaware of how quickly the code of my life is about to shatter.
